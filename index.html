Who is it for?

Spotify Scopes is meant to connect people who are interested in creating and sharing generative art. It is also meant to give people who may not be interested in programming a more immersive way to listen to music. Whether it be creating an exciting atmosphere for a party or a chill vibe for studying, Spotify Scopes will set the mood. For this project, I have created two user personas to contextualize my research.


Pete is a third year graphic design student who became interested in programming and generative art through exploring Javascript in his Web Design class. He loves music and started using his coding skills to make visuals for his favorite music. He uses Spotify and wished there was a way to connect it to his projects.

Harold is 23 years old. He has a group of friends who he grew up with and they have a lot of weekend get togethers at his house. When the night winds down they usually take turns playing new music and music videos on the TV. This is their weekly routine and Harold is looking for something new and interesting to show his friends and keep the party alive.

Week One: General Research

In order to develop my concept, two of my colleagues and I, Rory and Beni, researched different Algorithm based features for streaming apps and decided that Spotify was the right choice because it offered the most room for customization.

daylist

Creates a playlist based on your listening histoy, based on the times of the day you listen to it. Creates a creative title and allows you to share playlists with friends.

superfan

An external feature that uses your weekly data from Spotify to write a funny blurb about your listening history and shares it with your friends.

rewind

Uses your yearly listening data to show you your top artists for that year as well as other statistics. You can share it with friends and compare your listening history.

Something all of these features have in common is that they create some type of community surrounding music using an algorithm. I hadn't landed on a concept after conducting our research and was feeling lost. That is, until I remembered an algoithm based music application from my childhood: Visualizations for Windows Media Player. Upon doing further research, I realized there was an entire community surrounding Windows media player and developing different visualizer skins for it. Unfortunately, windows disabled the visualization feature after Windows 10 and on top of that, I have a Mac. I found this extremely interesting and wanted to run with the concept.

Week Five: Advertisement

During week five I planned my advertisement for Spotify Scopes. I wanted to focus on Harold's user problem for the video because it's a more relateable issue for the majority of Spotify users. I still included a scene of the main character airdropping a Javascript file to his phone because the creation of a community for creative coders is an important part of the story.

Proof of concept

for this project, I wanted to create an actual audio visualizer to prove that this could be done. The visualizer uses the getByteFrequencyData() instance method to convert the audio frequency data into an array. Each item in the array represents the decible value for a given frequency that is then animated on an html canvas.
